Problem → Solution
❌ The Problem
92% of people abandon their goals within 6 weeks. Habit apps have the worst retention in consumer tech — the average user opens Habitica 3x, then never again. Free streaks and badges create zero accountability. There's no cost to failure.
"The problem with every habit app I've tried is that when I skip a day, nothing happens." — User interview, March 2026
✅ The Solution
Ante puts real money on the line. Set a daily stake ($1–$100), track your habit, and miss a day → your stake goes to your chosen charity. Loss aversion — the strongest known motivator — does the work. We don't compete with Headspace. We make Headspace work.
"I've done my 5am runs for 60 days straight. There's no way I'm letting $25 go to a cause I hate." — @jakethakkar

Why Now
  • Gen Z treats self-improvement like a sport — #75Hard, Huberman Protocol, biohacking have normalized structured, high-stakes self-discipline. Our cohort is primed.
  • Mental health apps peaked and plateaued — Calm and Headspace are growing single digits. Users want outcomes, not content.
  • Apple Pay / Stripe have made micro-transactions frictionless — Collecting $10 used to require a bank integration. Now it's a single SDK call.
  • Behavioral economics is now mainstream — "Loss aversion" is on TikTok. Users arrive understanding the mechanic, reducing education cost.
  • No well-funded competitor — Beeminder is bootstrapped. StickK raised $6M in 2009 and hasn't shipped mobile-first. The incumbent is a 15-year-old desktop app.
